Matchmaking

Rather than a traditional dating matchmaking app, we decided to go down the route of matching gamers together. Although a lot of games do automatic matchmaking, players prefer to play with friends and/or people they know. They are less likely to be 'toxic' and more likely to have an enjoyable experience and return to the game.Our app functions almost as a pre-lobby lobby and so we aimed to provide similar funcitonality.

Our initial target games were MOBA's due to their in-game systems that push players, no matter what skill level, to work as a team, each with a different role. Metting players outside of the game through our app could potentially provide them with the same connection as a dating app would.

A big difference with our approach compared to a traditional approach is that we are matching up to five people at once where as most apps would be a one-to-one match. Our main conditions are play area and time available, latency is a huge deal in online video games and in a large country such as Australia, timezones also play a big part. We also aimed to have a kick feature in, something present in most multiplayer games lobby systems. Another matching criteria is age-range, we delibrately kept this broad to protect children and would keep this information private as so to try and avoid a situation like Roblox.
